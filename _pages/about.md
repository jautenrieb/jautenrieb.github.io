I’m **Johannes**, an aerospace researcher with a deep interest in safety-critical guidance and control methods for complex flight systems. I am a **Research Scientist** at the [Institute of Flight Systems](https://www.dlr.de/en/ft), **German Aerospace Center (DLR)**, where I work on advanced control and optimization methods for aerospace applications. My research focuses on the safe control of nonlinear systems in uncertain environments, with particular emphasis on robust adaptive control methods, control algorithms for state-constrained systems with formal guarantees, multi-agent guidance and control, and optimization-based control architectures.

During my research at DLR, I had the opportunity to visit the [AAC-Lab](http://aaclab.mit.edu/index.php) at the **Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), USA**, in 2022–23, where I worked with **Dr. Annaswamy** on control barrier functions for safety-critical and adaptive flight control systems. In 2024, I also joined the [Control Group](https://eng.ox.ac.uk/control/) at the **University of Oxford, UK**, where I worked with **Dr. Jack Umenberger** on robust online optimization algorithms for dynamical systems operating under uncertainty.

Before joining DLR, I completed an **M.Sc.** in **Autonomous Vehicle Dynamics and Control** at **Cranfield University, UK**, where I worked on flight control for eVTOL air taxis, including projects in collaboration with **Rolls-Royce** in the UK and at the **Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST), South Korea**. Prior to that, I earned a **B.Eng.** in **Aerospace Engineering** from the **University of Applied Sciences Aachen, Germany**.
